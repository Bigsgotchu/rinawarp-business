2. CORE ETHICAL PRINCIPLES

2.1 Transparency

We believe users have a right to understand how AI systems affect them. We commit to:

  • Clearly disclosing when AI is being used
  • Providing understandable explanations of AI decisions
  • Maintaining public documentation of our AI systems
  • Being honest about AI capabilities and limitations

2.2 Fairness and Non-Discrimination

AI systems must not discriminate or perpetuate harmful biases. We:

  • Regularly audit our AI systems for bias
  • Use diverse training data when possible
  • Provide mechanisms for users to report biased outputs
  • Design systems that work equitably across different user groups

2.3 Privacy and Data Protection

User privacy is paramount. Our AI systems:

  • Minimize data collection to what's necessary
  • Use data only for stated purposes
  • Implement strong security measures
  • Respect user consent and control

2.4 Safety and Security

AI must be developed and used safely. We ensure:

  • Robust testing before deployment
  • Monitoring for harmful outputs
  • Quick response to safety issues
  • Secure AI infrastructure

2.5 Human Oversight

Humans remain in control of AI systems. We maintain:

  • Human review of high-risk AI outputs
  • Ability for users to override AI decisions
  • Clear boundaries on AI autonomy
  • Human accountability for AI actions
